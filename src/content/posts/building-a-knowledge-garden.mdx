## Two kinds of writing

A conventional blog treats every piece of writing as a dated item in one stream. That works for essays, but it is awkward for material that grows lesson by lesson.

This site keeps two related systems:

1. **Posts** are complete arguments or stories.
2. **Subjects** are maps that gather many lessons into a navigable whole.

<Callout type="note" title="The organizing principle">
  Dates answer *when was this written?* Structure answers *where does this idea belong?* A useful knowledge site needs both.
</Callout>

## Content stays portable

The source remains Markdown by default. MDX is available only when an idea benefits from a richer component such as a gallery, callout, or embedded video.
