## Friction is not always the enemy

The best tool is not necessarily the one with the most features. A little constraint can protect attention by making the next action obvious.

## What plain text gets right

Plain text is searchable, portable, and easy to version. It is also honest: the value has to come from the words and their structure.

> A durable note is one you can still open after the application around it disappears.

## A working rule

Choose the smallest tool that preserves the important part of the work. Add complexity only when a real limitation appears, not when a hypothetical feature sounds appealing.
