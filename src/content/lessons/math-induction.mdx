## The idea

Mathematical induction proves that a statement holds for every integer from a chosen starting point onward.

1. Prove the **base case**.
2. Assume the statement is true for an arbitrary integer `k`.
3. Use that assumption to prove the statement for `k + 1`.

<Callout title="Key point" type="tip">
  The induction hypothesis is an assumption used inside the inductive step, not a conclusion that may be used without explanation.
</Callout>

## A small example

For positive integers `n`, the sum from `1` to `n` is:

```text
1 + 2 + ... + n = n(n + 1) / 2
```
